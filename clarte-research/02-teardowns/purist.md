# Purist — Teardown

## 1. Brand Snapshot
**Positioning:** *"#1 Transparent Skincare Brand in Pakistan — A Science-Backed Skincare."* Lab-formulated, ingredient-led, dermatologically-guided. The pitch is INCI-on-the-bottle transparency + clinical actives without a doctor face. Closer to "The Ordinary–style transparency brand for Pakistan" than to a derm-clinic brand.
**Hero category:** Single-ingredient actives + targeted concern bundles. Heavy on retinol, hyaluronic acid, niacinamide, vitamin C, glycolic, salicylic, alpha arbutin, kojic.
**Price tier (3 PKR anchor prices):** Hyaluronic Acid Serum **Rs. 1,199** (from Rs. 1,600), Retinol Serum **Rs. 1,299** (from Rs. 1,650), Complete Anti-Aging System bundle **Rs. 5,199** (from Rs. 9,200). Sits in the **mass-local Rs. 750–2,395 band** flagged in Clarte's matrix — confirmed as the right brand.
**Origin:** Pakistan; D2C Shopify (purist.pk). No founder face surfaced — brand-as-protagonist, not person-led.
**Where sold:** purist.pk primary; active IG/TikTok/FB/YouTube; phone 0315-3891141; support@purist.pk. COD implied (standard PK).

- **Headline:** *"#1 Transparent Skincare Brand in Pakistan"* — category-claim, not benefit.
- **Subhead / proof:** *"A Science Backed Skincare — formulations created in controlled lab settings with dermatological guidance."*
- **Hero visual:** lab/scientist imagery, beaker shots, ingredient renderings — clinical without a clinic.
- **CTA:** Shop bestsellers / Shop by Ingredient / Shop by Concern (three parallel entry points).
- **Scroll depth:** promo bar → hero claim → SPF duo deal → bestsellers → "Shop by Ingredient" rail → "Shop by Concern" rail → bundle rail → reviews → IG UGC strip.

## 11. Clarte MD Strategic Implications

**Is Purist a real competitor or noise?** Conceptually adjacent, commercially not yet. Purist is the **closest brand to Clarte's "clinical actives" claim** in the local mass tier — they share vocabulary (retinol %, niacinamide, glycolic, "dermatologically guided," "lab-formulated") and they have a real 43% component-math bundle that mirrors Clarte's "perceived value-add" thesis. **But** they sit at Rs. 1,199–1,299 per serum vs Clarte's Rs. 2,000–2,500 — a different wallet entirely. They are not stealing Clarte's customer today; they are **training Clarte's future customer** to expect ingredient transparency and percentage labelling at half the price. The threat is **upward drift**: if Purist adds a doctor or a PMDC partner and raises one SKU into the Rs. 2,000+ band, they become a direct competitor overnight. They have the IA, the ingredient vocabulary, and the bundle math to do it. Treat as **medium-term threat, low-term substitution risk.**

**Three moves Clarte must make to differentiate:**

1. **Own the doctor/PMDC moat now, loudly.** Purist's single biggest gap is no named dermatologist and no PMDC badge — they claim "dermatologically guided" without evidence. Clarte's hero must lead with a *named, credentialed PMDC dermatologist* (face + license number + clinic). Not "doctor-trusted." Specifically *"Formulated by Dr. [Name], PMDC #[xxxx], in [Clinic]."* This is the one credential Purist's brand-as-protagonist model can't easily retrofit without re-anchoring its identity.

2. **Win the bundle math war on different terms — story, not steeper discount.** Purist's 43% bundle saving is real and beats Clarte's 8–12% perceived value-add on raw numbers. Clarte should **not** chase the percentage. Instead, reframe the Rs. 5,000 Radiance Ritual as a *clinic protocol*, not a discounted cart: "the 4-week regimen Dr. [Name] gives in-clinic, now at home." Purist sells "save Rs. 4,001"; Clarte sells "Dr. [Name]'s protocol — Rs. 5,000." Different category. Bundle PDP must include a one-page protocol PDF, a 28-day calendar, and a WhatsApp check-in — value-adds Purist's transparency-brand frame doesn't fit.

3. **Counter ingredient-transparency theatre with clinical-result evidence.** Purist's whole brand is "we tell you the ingredients." Clarte cannot win on that turf — they'd just look like a more expensive Purist. Pivot to *outcomes*: before/afters from real clinic patients (with consent), 4-week protocol photos, and concentration claims with the **clinical reasoning** ("we use 0.3% retinol because at 1% the irritation rate at 4 weeks in our patient cohort was 22%"). Purist lists ingredients; Clarte explains the clinical decision behind each one. This converts the price gap from a weakness into proof-of-care.
