# Kabishah — Teardown

## 1. Brand Snapshot
- **Positioning:** "Kabishah by Shahzad" is a mid-market Pakistani DTC skincare line that wraps locally manufactured actives in international-style certification language ("Certified Halaal", "RICI Certified", "ISO 9001 Certified", "Vegan Friendly") and sells almost exclusively at 40-50% off — value-tier pricing dressed up with premium-looking trust badges.
- **Hero category:** Brightening / pigmentation / anti-acne actives (serums, creams, sunblock) sold heavily as multi-product bundles.
- **Price tier:** Skin Remodeling Niacinamide Day Serum Rs. 1,132 (from Rs. 2,200); Advanced Repair Retinol Night Serum Rs. 1,132 (from Rs. 2,200); Vitamin C Serum Rs. 1,172 (from Rs. 2,100); Anti Acne Serum Rs. 1,224 (from Rs. 2,350). Bundles span Rs. 1,015 – Rs. 4,005. Sits squarely in the cheap-local band, *below* AccuFix/Simplist street pricing on individual SKUs.
- **Origin claim:** "Small-batch manufacturing for freshness", "Safe and ethically-sourced ingredients free of harsh chemicals", "products developed with consultation from dermatologists". Pakistan-based (+92 WhatsApp, @kabishahpk handles). Founding year not present on the page.
- **Where sold:** Direct-to-consumer via kabishah.com only; no retail partners disclosed. Heavy social presence (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Snapchat, WhatsApp +923234444215).

## 11. Clarte MD Strategic Implications
**Steal (2-3):**
1. **The four-badge certification stack** ("Certified Halaal / RICI / ISO 9001 / Vegan Friendly") — Kabishah proves these resonate with the Lahore mid-market. Clarte should out-badge them: add PMDC-registered dermatologist + GMP/manufacturer name + clinical-trial line on top of halal.
2. **Concern-named bundles** ("Special Pigmentation Bundle", "Melasma Bundle - 24 Hours Protection", "Anti Acne Bundle") — Kabishah names bundles by *problem*, not by product list. Clarte's "Radiance Ritual" is generic; consider problem-led variants (e.g. "Pigmentation Reset Ritual").
3. **5-item bundle as anchor SKU** — Kabishah's flagship All-Day Glow Set (5 SKUs, Rs. 4,005) maps cleanly onto Clarte's Rs. 5,000 hero. Confirms the 5-item structure converts.

**Avoid (1-2):**
1. **Discount-as-headline.** Leading with "Flat 26% OFF" plus per-bundle 37-50% strikes makes the brand read as discount-house, not premium. Clarte must hold price and lead on outcome/expertise.
2. **Backorder messaging on hero SKUs.** Both bundles and the hero serum are "backordered" — actively kills conversion. Clarte must launch with stock depth.

**Whitespace / threat assessment:**
- **Threat: Low-to-medium.** Kabishah owns sub-Rs. 2,500 bundles and the halal-certified niche. They sit *below* Clarte's Rs. 5,000 price band — different wallet conversation.
- **Whitespace Clarte can own:** (a) named clinical expert / PMDC dermatologist authorship, (b) before-after evidence (Kabishah has none), (c) review density (Kabishah's Loox widget appears empty/hidden), (d) premium unboxing + in-stock reliability, (e) full-price discipline with a *tight* 8-12% bundle value-add story rather than 40%+ discounting that trains buyers to wait for sales.
